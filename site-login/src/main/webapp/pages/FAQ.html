FAQ

Q: What are the Developer-provided Testing Services (DTS) hosted on the ONC Standard Implementation and Testing Environment (SITE?)

A: DTS are intended to facilitate health information interoperability by providing the resources and contact information for certified EHR vendors who voluntarily provide testing services for implementers and providers to test their implementation of the Direct Transport Standard. Detailed information about ONC DTS can be found at http://sitenv.org/direct-vendor-tools/.

Q: I am a service provider interested in offering our Direct Testing Service to implementers and providers. How do I register our service?

A: Please click on the Developer Registration Tab on the Developer Provided Testing Services page http://sitenv.org/direct-vendor-tools/ and click on the Registration Instructions link to download detailed instructions.

Q: How do I use the services provided on SITE to perform a Direct Transport Test?

A: Please navigate to http://sitenv.org/direct-vendor-tools/ and click on the “instructions” link to download the instructions regarding how to use SITE DTS services.

Q: How to I obtain a trust anchor?

A: SITE provides links to download the trust anchors for test service providers who are NOT members of DirectTrust.org. For those who are members of DirectTrust.org, their trust anchors can be found at http://DirectTrust.org Web Site Disclaimers.

Q: How do I schedule a Direct test with a testing service provider?

A: Please contact your service provider of choice using the Point of Contact email address provided on the DTS page (http://sitenv.org/direct-vendor-tools/) and schedule a time with the service provider to perform the test. The service provider can provide you with more detailed information.

Q: Do I have to pay to participate with DirectTrust.org test service providers?

A: DirectTrust.org offers various services and membership opportunities. Membership information can be found at https://www.directtrust.org/membership/Web Site Disclaimers ; however, you can download the interoperability testing bundle that contains trust anchors for the listed Interop Testing Bundle Members at https://services.directtrust.org/interoperability-testing-bundle/ Web Site Disclaimers.

Q: Is DTS a replacement for the NIST EHR Randomizer?

A: No. The NIST EHR Randomizer previously provided “CMS designated EHRs” for Direct Transport Testing as an option in order to meet Objective 3 of Meaningful Use Stage 2. Effective July 1st, 2015, CMS has discontinued this service (https://questions.cms.gov/faq.php?faqId=11666&id=5005.)

Q: When do I need to conduct the exchange in order to be eligible for the reporting period time frame?

A: Please find detailed instructions at https://questions.cms.gov/faq.php?faqId=11666&id=5005

Q: Can DTS services offered on SITE be used to meet Objective 3 of Meaningful Use Stage 2?

A: Not directly. Please find instructions on how to meet Objective 3 of Meaningful Use Stage 2 at https://questions.cms.gov/faq.php?faqId=11666&id=5005.

Q: With the EHR Randomizer no longer available, what EHR product should I use for meeting Objective #3 of Meaningful Use Stage 2?

Please find detailed instructions at https://questions.cms.gov/faq.php?faqId=11666&id=5005.

Q: Where can I find detailed instructions regarding meeting the Objective 3 of Meaningful Use Stage 2?

Please find detailed instructions at https://questions.cms.gov/faq.php?faqId=11666&id=5005.
